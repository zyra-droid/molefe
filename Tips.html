1. Start Small & Scale Up

Begin with a manageable farm size. Focus on learning crop cycles, soil management, and customer demand before expanding.

2. Know Your Market

Understand what sells in your area. For Maseru, fresh vegetables and eggs have high demand, while meat can be supplied from farther farms.

3. Diversify Products

Don't rely on one crop or livestock. Mix vegetables, poultry, and dairy to balance risks and increase income streams.

4. Track Finances

Record every expense and income. Use a simple ledger or farming app to calculate profit and manage cash flow effectively.

5. Build Good Relationships

Work with local suppliers and customers. A good network ensures you always have buyers and reliable input sources.

6. Use Technology

Leverage mobile apps for weather forecasts, farming techniques, and online markets to stay competitive and save time.
